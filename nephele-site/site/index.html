The Core Insight

Every other approach dries the droplets. We don't.

Every marine cloud brightening proposal to date follows the same chain: spray seawater, evaporate the droplets to dry salt crystals, and wait for boundary layer turbulence to loft them to cloud base over 30 to 60 minutes. Each step loses particles. Evaporative cooling at the source actually fights vertical transport.

Nephele injects seawater spray into the warm, saturated post-scrubber exhaust plume. The droplets never dry. The marine boundary layer stays above the NaCl deliquescence point at all altitudes up to cloud base. The droplets ride the buoyant plume upward as concentrated salt solution, arriving at cloud base as effectively pre-activated condensation nuclei. No drying. No deliquescence barrier. No activation delay.

The plume does the hard work for free.

The V-shaped transit

At the stack, injected droplets enter warm saturated air. They cannot evaporate. As the plume rises and entrains ambient air, the droplets concentrate somewhat but never crystallize, because open-ocean humidity in stratocumulus regions stays above 75%. As they approach cloud base, ambient humidity climbs back toward saturation and the droplets re-absorb water, arriving nearly at their original size.

The mid-transit concentration is actually beneficial: smaller droplets settle more slowly and coagulate less, making them easier to transport. And because they remain as solution throughout, every droplet that reaches cloud base is ready to participate in cloud physics immediately.

The Approach

Off-the-shelf hardware. Existing ships. The plume is the delivery system.

A bolt-on module mounts above the scrubber exhaust stack. Air-assist nozzles spray seawater onto splash impaction plates, shattering droplets into the sub-micron range. A mist eliminator above passes only CCN-active particles into the plume. The scrubber's own seawater supply feeds the system. No new fleet. No precision nanoparticle engineering.

01

Spray and shatter

Air-assist nozzles atomize filtered seawater onto splash impaction plates. The impact fragments parent droplets into daughters in the 0.3 to 3 micron range. Splash plate atomization is a proven industrial technology used continuously in kraft recovery boilers for decades.

02

Mist eliminator selects by physics

A standard off-the-shelf mist eliminator, the same type already in every marine scrubber, passes only sub-micron to few-micron droplets. Everything larger is caught and drains back through the scrubber. No precision filtration. No clogging. 97 to 99% open space, negligible pressure drop.

03

The plume delivers

Warm, saturated post-scrubber exhaust carries the solution droplets upward. They stay liquid throughout transit. The buoyant plume reaches cloud base altitude in minutes, not the hour required by passive lofting. Delivery efficiency is estimated at 10 to 100 times higher than ambient sea-level spraying.

04

Clouds brighten

Solution droplets activate as cloud condensation nuclei at very low supersaturations, with no deliquescence barrier to overcome. Cloud droplet number increases. Reflectivity increases. Ship tracks form.

05

Measured, verified, reversible

CERES/MODIS satellite data verifies radiative forcing. Replaceable mist eliminator cassettes provide a physical audit trail and independent scrubber compliance verification. Stop injection and the effect ceases within 48 hours. We seek to operate under full IMO MEPC oversight.

Why This Works

Not making new clouds. Restoring lost ship tracks.

Not new

We are restoring an effect that shipping itself produced for over a century. The physics has been understood for decades. The 2020 sulfur cap is a natural A/B test proving it works.

Not permanent

Salt aerosol atmospheric lifetime is hours to days. Stop injecting and cloud brightening halts within 48 hours. The fastest off switch in climate intervention.

Not sulfur

NaCl replaces SO₂ with twice the hygroscopicity. Inert in the atmosphere, naturally present in the ocean. The clean air benefits of IMO 2020 are fully preserved.

Controllable

Unlike SO₂, which reacted unpredictably in the atmosphere, NaCl is chemically inert. What we inject is what arrives at cloud base. Predictable, tunable, verifiable.

4,000+ ships ready

Scrubber-equipped vessels already have the seawater plumbing, exhaust infrastructure, and maintenance access. Installation during a routine port call. ~$250K per vessel.

Satellite-verified

NASA's CERES instruments provide independent, auditable radiative forcing measurement. Every watt is accounted for. Mist eliminator cassettes add a physical audit trail.

The Honest Picture

Current hardware reaches ~30% of pre-2020 levels. That's the starting point, not the ceiling.

Off-the-shelf air-assist nozzles with splash atomization deliver approximately 30% of a pre-2020 ship's effective cloud condensation nuclei at cloud base. Combined with the ship's own residual post-2020 aerosol, a Nephele-equipped vessel restores roughly half of the pre-2020 cloud perturbation. That is enough to demonstrate the concept, validate the verification methodology, and build the regulatory track record.

Reaching full parity requires nozzle technology optimized for CCN production in marine environments. That hardware does not yet exist as a commercial product, because the market for it does not yet exist.

IMO recognition of ship track restoration as an eligible activity under the Net-Zero Framework creates that market. It provides the commercial incentive for the spray engineering sector to develop fleet-ready hardware. Nephele's platform is ready for those nozzles the moment they arrive. The regulatory framework that incentivizes ship track restoration will also fund the hardware development that perfects it.
